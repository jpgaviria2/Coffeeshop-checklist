🍵 Matcha & Tea Preparation Guide

Traditional matcha preparation using Domo Tea products

⚠️ CRITICAL: Water Temperature
  • NEVER use boiling water - it will make the matcha bitter
  • Target temperature: 175°F (80°C)
  • Let water cool for 1 minute after boiling
  • Or use hot water tap and let cool 30 seconds

🍵 Traditional Matcha Tea (No Milk)

Water Ratios for Traditional Matcha

Strength Matcha Powder Hot Water (175°F) Notes
Light / Mild ½ - 1 tsp 2-3 oz (60-90ml) Subtle flavor, good for beginners
Standard / Medium 1-1½ tsp 3-4 oz (90-120ml) Balanced, traditional strength
Strong / Bold 1½-2 tsp 2-3 oz (60-90ml) Rich, intense matcha flavor

Step-by-Step: Traditional Matcha

  1. Prepare water: Heat water to 175°F (80°C). If boiling, let cool for 1 minute.
  2. Sift matcha (optional): Use small sieve to remove clumps. (Can skip if powder is fresh)
  3. Add powder to bowl/cup: Measure matcha powder based on desired strength (see table above)
  4. Add hot water: Pour 2-4 oz of 175°F water over matcha powder
  5. Whisk vigorously: Use bamboo whisk or handheld frother
    • If using bamboo whisk: Move in fast M or W motion for 15-30 seconds
    • If using frother: Whisk until fully mixed and frothy
  6. Check consistency: Should have a layer of foam on top, no clumps visible
  7. Serve immediately: Enjoy hot, or add ice for iced matcha tea

🥛 Matcha Latte (With Milk)

Matcha Latte Recipe Chart

Size Matcha Powder Hot Water (for whisking) Steamed Milk Total Volume
12oz 1-2 tsp 2 oz (60ml) 300ml ~12oz
16oz 2 tsp 2 oz (60ml) 415ml ~16oz

Step-by-Step: Hot Matcha Latte

  1. Measure matcha: Add powder to cup (1-2 tsp for 12oz, 2 tsp for 16oz)
  2. Add small amount of hot water: Pour 2 oz (60ml) of 175°F water into cup
  3. Whisk matcha paste: Whisk vigorously with bamboo whisk or frother until smooth (no clumps!)
  4. Steam milk: Steam 300ml (12oz) or 415ml (16oz) milk to 140-150°F
  5. Pour milk over matcha: Slowly add steamed milk to whisked matcha
  6. Stir gently: Mix to combine, creating light foam on top
  7. Serve: Top with light dusting of matcha powder (optional)

Step-by-Step: Iced Matcha Latte

  1. Measure matcha: Add powder to separate mixing container (1-2 tsp for 12oz, 2 tsp for 16oz)
  2. Whisk with cold milk: Add small amount of cold milk (2-3 oz) and whisk until smooth paste forms
  3. Fill cup with ice: Fill cup 3/4 full with ice
  4. Add matcha mixture: Pour whisked matcha paste over ice
  5. Add remaining cold milk: Pour 300ml (12oz) or 415ml (16oz) cold milk
  6. Stir well: Mix thoroughly to ensure matcha is evenly distributed
  7. Serve: With straw

🛠️ Tools & Equipment

Required Tools
  • Bamboo whisk (chasen): Traditional tool for making matcha. Use M or W motion.
  • OR handheld frother: Electric alternative, whisk until smooth and frothy
  • Small sieve (optional): Removes clumps from powder
  • Measuring spoons: For accurate matcha portions
  • Thermometer (optional): To verify water temperature

💡 Pro Tips

Matcha Whisking Technique
  • M or W motion: Move whisk quickly in these patterns - this aerates the matcha and creates foam
  • Speed matters: Whisk briskly for 15-30 seconds, not gently
  • No clumps: If clumps appear, keep whisking or use sieve next time
  • Froth goal: Aim for a layer of fine bubbles on top
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Water too hot: Makes matcha bitter and destroys nutrients. Always use 175°F, NOT boiling!
  • Not whisking enough: Results in clumpy, uneven drink. Whisk vigorously!
  • Adding powder directly to milk: Won't dissolve properly. Always whisk with water first.
  • Using old matcha: Fresh matcha is bright green. Dull/brown = stale.
Flavor Variations (Optional)
  • Add sweetener: Honey, vanilla syrup, or simple syrup (ask customer preference)
  • Vanilla matcha latte: Add 1 pump vanilla syrup before adding milk
  • Cinnamon matcha: Light dusting of cinnamon on foam
  • Iced with cream: Top iced matcha latte with cold cream float
