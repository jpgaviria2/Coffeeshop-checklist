🍥 Cinnamon Buns

Overnight prep, baking & temperature guide

🌡️ Key Rule: Cinnamon buns are done by internal temperature, not time. Target: 93°C (199°F)

Night Before — Prep (Closing Shift, ~3:00 PM)

Step 1: Tray Setup
  • Take cinnamon buns from the freezer
  • Place 8–9 buns per tray
  • Space them well — they expand overnight
  • Make sure buns are NOT touching each other
Step 2: Overnight Thaw
  • Leave trays in the oven overnight (oven OFF)
  • Buns need ~18 hours to fully defrost and rise
  • That's why we prep by 3:00 PM — ready by morning
💡 Spacing matters! If buns are too close, they'll grow into each other overnight and stick together. 8–9 per tray with good gaps is the sweet spot.

Morning — Baking (After Pastries)

Step 3: Preheat Oven
  • Set oven to 275°F
  • Wait for oven to reach temperature
Step 4: Bake with Lid (20 min)
  • Place the tray on the top shelf
  • Put the lid/cover ON the tray
  • Bake for 20 minutes with lid on
  • The lid traps moisture so buns stay soft and rise evenly
Step 5: Remove Lid & Finish (12 min)
  • At 20 minutes: remove the lid
  • Continue baking uncovered for 12 more minutes
  • This lets the tops brown and caramelize
Step 6: Check Temperature
  • Use the probe thermometer
  • Insert into the center of a bun
  • Target: 93°C (199°F)
  • If not at temp, bake a few more minutes and check again
  • Let cool briefly before serving
✅ Quick Summary:
275°F → Top shelf with lid → 20 min covered → Remove lid → 12 min uncovered → Check for 93°C inside → Done!

Troubleshooting

Common Issues
  • Buns stuck together? → Space them further apart next time (max 8 per tray)
  • Doughy inside? → Internal temp wasn't 93°C. Always check with probe!
  • Too dry/hard? → Make sure lid was on for the first 20 min
  • Didn't rise enough? → May not have thawed long enough. Need ~18 hours overnight.
