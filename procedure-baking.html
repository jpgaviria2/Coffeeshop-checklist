Prep (Night Before)

Evening Setup
  • Remove pastries from freezer
  • Place on EDGES of trays (not center!)
  • Space them apart for even thawing
  • Place trays in oven (OFF) overnight
  • Close oven door

Morning Baking

Step 1: Preheat Oven
  • Remove pastries from oven
  • Set oven to 375¬∞F
  • Wait for indicator light to turn off (oven ready)
Step 2: Prepare Egg Wash
  • Crack one egg into small bowl
  • Stir/beat with fork until uniform
  • Do NOT apply yet! Wait until oven is ready
  • Use red-tipped brush for application
Step 3: Apply Egg Wash
  • When oven light turns off, egg wash is ready
  • Lightly paint each pastry with egg (thin coat)
  • Cover all exposed surfaces
  • Don't over-apply (causes burning)
Step 4: Load Oven
  • Use ONLY 2 trays:
    • 1st position from bottom
    • 3rd position from bottom
  • Remove 2nd and top tray (place on top of oven)
  • Ensure good spacing between trays
Step 5: First Bake
  • Close oven door
  • Set timer for 10 minutes
  • Do NOT open door during this time
Step 6: Rotate & Separate
  • After 10 minutes, open door
  • Shake trays gently to separate pastries from paper
  • Separate any touching pastries
  • Rotate both trays 180¬∞
  • Return to same rack positions
Step 7: Final Bake
  • Set timer for 4-5 minutes
  • Check at 14 minutes total time
  • Done when: Golden brown, puffed up
  • Remove immediately when ready
üìù Pro Tip: Total bake time is usually 14-15 minutes. Check at 14 min - if golden brown, they're done!

Quality Checks

  • Color: Golden brown (not pale, not dark)
  • Texture: Flaky layers visible, puffed
  • Sound: Should feel crispy when tapped
  • Bottom: Check underside - should be golden too

Troubleshooting

  • Pastries pale after 15 min? Oven not hot enough, increase 10¬∞F
  • Burning on edges? Too much egg wash OR oven too hot
  • Not puffed? Didn't thaw properly overnight
  • Soggy bottom? Oven not preheated fully OR too crowded
